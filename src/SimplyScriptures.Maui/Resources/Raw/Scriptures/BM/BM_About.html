ABOUT THIS EDITION This edition increases the readability of The Book of Mormon by modernizing the page layout. The text is put into standard paragraphs and white space and other modern tools (section headers, bullet lists, etc.) are used to help with readability. While the verses and chapters of the 1981 edition have been maintained to help locate specific verses, those references are visually subdued so as to not distract from the message. Words that are part of the 1981 scriptural record appear in serif font in the main body. Anything not part of that record (verse numbers, chapter numbers, and section headers) appear in this sans-serif font. Anything in a sans-serif font is an addition of men and can be treated accordingly. It should also be noted that there was no punctuation in the manuscript that was delivered to the printer. The punctuation in The Book of Mormon was added by the original typesetter, not Joseph Smith. Accordingly, this edition occasionally makes changes to the punctuation. Bible quotations are designated by a shaded background and the reference scripture is shown in the margin. When Nephi, Mormon, or Moroni interrupts the history to give commentary or background information, it is designated by a box. This edition highlights some of the textual changes that have been made over time. Brigham Young University’s The Book of Mormon Critical Text Project was used as the basis for determining changes. Many of these changes were simple scribal errors between the original manuscript, printer’s manuscript, and printed versions. A few others were deliberate changes that Joseph Smith made after 1830. Where there have been changes, the words found in the 1981 edition are denoted by underline and the changes are shown in margin notes with the source indicated by: for the original manuscript. for the printer’s manuscript. for the similar passage in the Joseph Smith Translation of the Bible. There are sections of Isaiah in the Book of Mormon that have differences between both the KJV and JST versions of Isaiah. These unique phrases are denoted by dashed underline.
